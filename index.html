Businessman to Nature Activist: Life of Mr.Karaca

Hayrettin Karaca was born on the 4th of April 1922 in Bandirma, Turkey. His father and mother immigrated from Crimea. After graduating high-school he started to manage his family's textile factories and has grown the business to be one of the leading companies in the industry. He explains his early carrer path: "I didn't want to be a businessman, what I wanted was to study literature and be close to nature. However, back then, it was out of the question to go against your parents' wishes."

In his 50s, following his lifelong dreams, he founded a private arboretum. He gathered various plant seeds during his travels. Today his Karaca Arboretum is well respected and known internationaly. The arboratum houses over 14.000 plant species and is a center for gene preservation of endangered endemic plant species.

Mr Karaca was one of the founding members of TEMA Foundation, a non-profit for reforestation and prevention of erosion. He was an activist, who had immense impact on publicising conservation of nature.

"I have never met a person so dedicated to working for the benefit of humankind, all the while without pursuing any personal gain."

-- Franz H. Meyer, Professor of Ecology, University of Hannover

Some of his awards include:

  • 1992 - Global 500 Roll of Honour Award from United Nations Environment Program
  • 1993 - Environment Award from International Olympic Committee
  • 1994 - Melvin Jones Fellowship from Lions Clubs International
  • 1994- Outstanding Service Award from Ministry of Environment
  • 1997- Culture and Art Grand Award from Presidecy of the Republic
  • 2005 - Honour Award from the Turkish Parliament
  • 2012 - Right Livelihood Award
  • 2013 - The Forest Heroes Award from United Nations

Mr. Karaca passed away on January 20, 2020. His legacy will live on through his foundation and people who were touched by his love for nature and its inhabitants.
